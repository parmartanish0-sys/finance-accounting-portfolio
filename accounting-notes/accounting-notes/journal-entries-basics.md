# Journal Entries – Basics

This note explains the fundamentals of journal entries used in day-to-day
accounting work, with simple explanations and practical examples.

---

## What is a Journal Entry?

A journal entry is the first step of recording a financial transaction in
the accounting system. Every transaction affects at least two accounts.

---

## Debit and Credit – Simple Logic

- Debit (Dr) means **receiving value**
- Credit (Cr) means **giving value**

This depends on the type of account.

---

## Types of Accounts

1. Assets – What the business owns  
2. Liabilities – What the business owes  
3. Income – Earnings of the business  
4. Expenses – Costs incurred

---

## Common Journal Entry Examples

### 1. Purchase of Goods in Cash
Goods purchased for ₹10,000 in cash.
Purchases A/c Dr 10,000
To Cash A/c 10,000

---

### 2. Sales Made in Cash
Goods sold for ₹15,000 in cash.
Cash A/c Dr 15,000
To Sales A/c 15,000

---

### 3. Payment of Office Rent
Office rent paid ₹5,000.
Rent Expense A/c Dr 5,000
To Cash A/c 5,000

---

### 4. Salary Paid to Employees
Salary paid ₹20,000.
Salary Expense A/c Dr 20,000
To Cash A/c 20,000

---

## Why Journal Entries Matter in Jobs

- Basis of financial statements
- Used in reconciliations and audits
- Required for month-end closing
- Helps identify errors and adjustments
